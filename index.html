Why Coating System Selection Is the Most Critical Decision

The wrong coating on the wrong substrate fails in 12–18 months. The right system, applied at the correct mil thickness, lasts 7–15 years. Here's what separates a professional commercial coating contractor from a residential painter with a commercial license.

🔬

Substrate-Matched Systems

Concrete, CMU, stucco, EIFS, metal, and wood each require a different primer and topcoat system. We specify per substrate — not per price point.

📐

Correct Mil Thickness

We apply coatings at the manufacturer's specified dry film thickness (DFT). Under-applied coatings fail prematurely. We use wet film gauges on every project.

🌡️

Atlanta Climate Compliance

Atlanta's humidity, UV index, and temperature swings demand elastomeric and breathable coating systems. We select products rated for the Southeast's climate profile.

📋

TDS-Compliant Application

Every coating is applied per the manufacturer's Technical Data Sheet — correct surface temperature, humidity window, recoat interval, and application method. No shortcuts.

🛡️

Corrosion & Chemical Resistance

Industrial environments demand epoxy, urethane, or polyurea systems rated for chemical exposure, abrasion, and corrosion. We specify the right system for your facility's operating conditions.

📦

Original Packaging — Always

All materials are delivered to your project in original manufacturer packaging bearing the product name, number, and batch code. No repackaged or diluted materials — ever.

Our Commercial & Industrial Painting Process

Every project follows the same disciplined process — from site assessment to final walkthrough. No shortcuts. No surprises.

1

Site Assessment & Specification

On-site evaluation of substrate condition, surface area, access requirements, and coating system specification. Written scope of work provided before any work begins.

2

Surface Preparation

Pressure washing, abrasive blasting, power tool cleaning, caulking, patching, skim coating, and priming — completed to SSPC standards before any finish coat is applied.

3

Containment & Protection

Full masking, drop cloth protection, and containment setup to protect adjacent surfaces, equipment, vehicles, and occupied areas from overspray and debris.

4

Coating Application

Airless spray, roller, or brush application per TDS specifications. Wet film thickness checked throughout. Recoat intervals strictly observed for full system performance.

5

Final Inspection & Warranty

Joint walkthrough with your facility manager or PM. Punch list items addressed same day. Written workmanship warranty issued at project close.

Technical Q&A

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions facility managers and project owners ask most.

How long does a commercial exterior painting project take?

Timeline depends on building size, substrate condition, and weather windows. A typical 3–5 story commercial building takes 5–15 business days. We provide a detailed project schedule before mobilization so your operations team can plan accordingly.

Do you paint occupied commercial buildings?

Yes. We specialize in occupied-facility painting. We use low-VOC and zero-VOC coatings for interior work, schedule after-hours or weekend shifts, and section off work zones to maintain safe, uninterrupted business operations throughout the project.

What surface preparation do you perform before painting?

Surface prep is the foundation of a lasting finish. Depending on the substrate, we perform pressure washing, sandblasting, power tool cleaning of rust and flaking paint, caulking, crack patching, and priming. All prep follows SSPC and manufacturer specifications.

Do you handle sandblasting and industrial coatings?

Yes. We provide abrasive sandblasting for steel structures, tanks, and industrial equipment requiring bare-metal surface profiles. We apply high-performance industrial coatings including epoxy, polyurethane, and zinc-rich primers rated for corrosive and chemical environments.

What types of floor coatings do you install?

We install epoxy floor coatings, polyaspartic systems, urethane cement, and anti-slip broadcast systems for warehouses, manufacturing floors, loading docks, and commercial garages. We also apply warehouse line striping and safety markings per OSHA and ANSI standards.

Are you licensed, bonded, and insured in Georgia?

Yes. Southeast Commercial Services holds a Georgia contractor's license, carries $Multi-Million general liability insurance, and maintains workers' compensation coverage on all crew members. Certificates of insurance are available upon request for any project.

Do you work on high-rise buildings?

Yes. Our crews are trained and certified for elevated work including swing stage, suspended scaffolding, aerial lift, and rope access systems. We comply with all OSHA fall protection standards for work above 6 feet on commercial and high-rise structures.

How do you handle lead paint on older commercial buildings?

All work involving lead-containing coatings is conducted in full compliance with EPA RRP regulations and OSHA 1926.62. Our team is trained in lead-safe work practices, containment, and proper disposal — protecting your occupants, workers, and the environment.

What is the service area for Southeast Commercial Services?

We serve the greater Atlanta metro area including Fulton, DeKalb, Gwinnett, Cobb, Clayton, Cherokee, and Forsyth counties. We regularly work in Midtown, Buckhead, Downtown Atlanta, Sandy Springs, Alpharetta, Marietta, Smyrna, Decatur, and Norcross.
