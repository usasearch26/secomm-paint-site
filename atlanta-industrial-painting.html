Why Vendor Selection Matters

The Real Cost of a Failed Coating Project

When an industrial coating fails — because of inadequate surface prep, a mismatched product spec, or a contractor who walked after the check cleared — the problem isn't just a repaint. It's accelerated corrosion on structural steel that's now behind equipment. It's a failed FDA audit because floor coatings weren't food-safe compliant. It's OSHA floor markings that wear through in three months and put you in a liability position you have to explain to your safety director.

Facility managers who've been through a bad coating contractor know the pattern: low bid, rushed schedule, shortcuts on prep that show up 18 months later as peeling, blistering, or bare metal.

At Southeast, your production environment is the constraint — not our schedule. That means off-hours execution when needed, phased work in live facilities, documented prep for your records, and coating specs matched to what your surfaces actually face.

We've been called in to fix failed coating jobs more times than we can count. We'd rather be your first call than your second.

Risk Management

Lead-based paint preparation is conducted under full EPA RRP Rule compliance — worker protection, containment, waste handling, and post-project documentation. Rinse water, solvents, and unused coatings are disposed of per current AQMD and local ordinance requirements.

Coverage

Warranty on Every Project

One-year labor warranty on every project. If any workmanship issue surfaces within the first year, we address it promptly at no additional cost. Long-term manufacturer warranties apply to all coating products. Documentation provided at project close.

The labor warranty reflects confidence in how we prepare surfaces and apply coating systems. A coating that fails within 12 months almost always traces back to surface prep or application conditions — not the product. We control both, which is why we can stand behind the work.

Manufacturer warranties on Sherwin-Williams, PPG Amercoat, and Benjamin Moore systems add product-side coverage. Warranty terms vary by coating system and are disclosed in writing with each project proposal.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions — Industrial Painting in Metro Atlanta

What types of industrial facilities do you paint in Metro Atlanta?

We paint manufacturing plants, warehouses, food processing facilities, distribution centers, pharmaceutical operations, water treatment infrastructure, chemical plants, parking structures, and steel-frame commercial buildings throughout Metro Atlanta, Gainesville, Athens, and surrounding industrial corridors. If it has structural steel, concrete, corrugated metal siding, or industrial equipment requiring protective coatings, we have the crew and the systems for it.

How do you schedule work without disrupting our operations?

We build the project schedule around your production calendar, not ours. Off-hours, overnight windows, weekends, and planned plant shutdowns are all standard. We phase work in zones so adjacent operations stay live, coordinate directly with your facility manager or safety officer, and provide a detailed project schedule with every proposal so your team can plan accordingly. No surprise mobilizations.

How do you handle surface preparation for industrial painting?

Surface preparation is the single biggest determinant of how long any coating system lasts. Depending on substrate and spec requirements, we use abrasive blasting, mechanical grinding, power washing, chemical degreasing, caulking, and crack repair. Containment zones are secured and adjacent equipment or operations protected before prep begins. Every prep step is documented — you should have a record of what was done, not just what it looks like when we leave.

What coating systems and product brands do you use?

We work with Sherwin-Williams Industrial, PPG Amercoat, and Benjamin Moore commercial-grade coating systems — selected based on your facility's exposure conditions, not what's fastest to apply. Epoxy floor coatings for chemical and impact resistance. Urethane topcoats for gloss and UV stability. Polyaspartic systems for rapid return-to-service. Elastomeric sealers for exterior moisture management. FDA/USDA-compliant finishes for food and pharmaceutical environments. We spec what holds.

Are you licensed, insured, and Procore Verified?

Yes on all three. Southeast Commercial Services is a Georgia-registered business operating since 1991. We carry $2 million general liability insurance and full workers' compensation. We are Procore Verified — qualified through Procore's vetting process, the same platform most commercial and industrial facility managers use to manage projects. Certificates of insurance and Procore Verified documentation are available to your procurement or risk management team on request.

What does the quoting and site walk process look like?

We start with an on-site facility walk. An estimator visits your facility, assesses surface conditions, reviews substrate issues, discusses your operational constraints, and identifies compliance or hazardous material considerations upfront. From that walk we produce a line-item estimate covering surface preparation, coating system specification, labor, and a project schedule. No ballpark numbers. No commitment required for the site walk. We typically schedule visits within a few business days of your request.

What is your service area?

We serve Metro Atlanta and surrounding industrial corridors including the Fulton Industrial District, I-85 NE manufacturing zone, Gainesville, Athens, Decatur, Norcross, Smyrna, Kennesaw, Peachtree City, and the broader Georgia industrial market. Contact us to confirm coverage for your location.

Do you handle lead-based paint and hazardous material compliance?

Yes. All work involving lead-based coatings or hazardous substrates is conducted under full EPA RRP Rule compliance — worker protection, proper containment, waste handling, and post-project documentation. Rinse water, solvents, and unused materials are disposed of per AQMD and local ordinance. If your facility has known or suspected lead paint, we address it in the surface prep scope at project outset — not as a change order after work has started.
