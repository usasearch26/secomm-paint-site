QUESTIONS ENGINEERS & FACILITY MANAGERS ASK

Technical Q&A

Technical answers to common industrial abrasive blasting questions from facility managers and engineers across the Atlanta, GA metro area.

What SSPC blast standards do you work to, and how do I specify the right one for my coating system?

We perform blasting to SSPC SP-6 (Commercial Blast), SSPC SP-10 (Near-White Metal), and SSPC SP-5 (White Metal). The correct standard depends on your coating system's requirements and the service environment. For immersion service or high-performance coatings, SP-10 or SP-5 is typically required. We'll review your coating manufacturer's data sheet and recommend the appropriate standard — or work directly from your engineering spec.

How do you handle containment and environmental compliance during blasting?

We engineer containment systems appropriate to the project — from shrink-wrap enclosures and negative-pressure tenting to ground tarps and vacuum-assisted blasting for sensitive environments. All spent abrasive and debris is characterized and disposed of per Georgia EPD and EPA regulations. For lead-containing coatings, we follow OSHA 1926.62 and EPA RRP protocols.

Can you perform abrasive blasting inside our storage tanks while maintaining OSHA confined space compliance?

Yes. Our crews are trained and equipped for OSHA 29 CFR 1910.146 permit-required confined space entry. We provide full atmospheric monitoring (O₂, LEL, CO, H₂S), forced-air ventilation, communication systems, and rescue standby personnel. We coordinate the confined space permit process with your safety department before entry.

What types of industrial facilities do you typically serve?

We serve power generation facilities, water and wastewater treatment plants, petrochemical operations, manufacturing plants, food processing facilities, logistics hubs, and other heavy industrial environments. Our crews are built for commercial and industrial scopes where coating system performance, documentation, and safe execution matter.

Do you handle residential or small-scale sandblasting projects?

No. Southeast Commercial Services is strictly a commercial and industrial contractor. We do not take residential requests. That helps protect scheduling, maintain jobsite standards, and keep our crews focused on complex industrial scopes.
