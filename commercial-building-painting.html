What a Deteriorating Building Exterior Costs You — Before You Call Anyone

Faded paint, chalking masonry, rust-stained concrete panels, and peeling caulk aren't cosmetic problems — they're early-warning signs of envelope failure. Left unaddressed, moisture infiltration accelerates behind the surface, driving up remediation costs and creating liability exposure that lands on the facility manager's desk.

The other cost is tenant and client perception. A well-maintained building exterior signals operational competence. A building that looks overdue for attention signals the opposite — and in competitive Atlanta submarkets like Midtown, Buckhead, and Cumberland, that impression affects lease renewalsand vendor relationships.

Atlanta Commercial Building Painting — What the Scope Actually Covers

Every project starts with a site walk. We assess substrate condition, identify failed caulk joints, measure coating adhesion, and document surface defects before anything touches the wall.

Exterior Building Coating

Concrete masonry, stucco, EIFS, metal panel systems, tilt-wall, and brick — we apply elastomeric coatings, DTM (direct-to-metal) finishes, and acrylic topcoats rated for Atlanta's humidity and UV load. Surface prep includes pressure washing, crack repair, and caulking before any paint is applied.

Typical production rate: 4,000–8,000 sq ft per day depending on substrate condition and coating system.

Interior Commercial Repaint

Lobby-to-loading-dock interior repaints for office buildings, mid-rise buildings, retail centers, and industrial facilities. We work in occupied buildings — phased by floor or zone, with containment and daily cleanup to maintain normal operations throughout the project.

Low-VOC and zero-VOC coating options available for sensitive environments including medical offices and food-adjacent facilities.

Structural Steel & Metal

Exposed structural steel, steel stairwells, metal doors and frames, roof decking, and corrugated metal panels. We apply rust-inhibiting primers followed by industrial-grade topcoats per SSPC surface preparation standards.

Documentation available for all surface prep grades and coating systems applied — useful for capital improvement records and insurance carriers.

Commercial Building Painting — Questions Facility Managers Ask Us

Straight answers, no sales language.

How long does a commercial building painting project take?

It depends on square footage, substrate condition, number of coats, and access method. A typical 50,000 sq ft tilt-wall exterior runs 10–15 business days with a full crew. A 10-story office tower exterior can run 6–10 weeks depending on swing-stage access and weather windows. We provide a written schedule before mobilization — not a range, a specific projected completion date with contingency language clearly noted.

Can you paint while our building is occupied?

Yes. Occupied-building repaints are the majority of our commercial work. We phase by floor or wing, contain the work area with plastic barriers and negative pressure where needed, and conduct air quality checks before opening treated areas to building occupants. For exterior work on occupied buildings, we coordinate with property management on tenant notifications and swing-stage scheduling around operating hours.

What exterior coating systems do you use for Atlanta's climate?

Atlanta's combination of high UV, humidity, and freeze-thaw cycles in winter calls for elastomeric coatings on masonry and stucco — specifically 100% acrylic elastomeric systems rated for 400%+ elongation that bridge hairline cracks and resist moisture infiltration. For metal panel systems and structural steel, we use DTM (direct-to-metal) acrylic or alkyd finishes with a rust-inhibiting primer. All coatings are selected to meet current VOC limits and are applied per manufacturer recommendations for film thickness and dry times.

What surface prep is included — and what costs extra?

Standard scope includes pressure washing, surface cleaning, sanding of glossy areas, spot priming of bare substrate, and recaulking of deteriorated joints. Structural crack repair, stucco patching beyond minor hairlines, masonry waterproofing, and lead abatement are scoped and priced separately after the site walk — we document these as line items so you can review and approve before we proceed. No surprises during the project.

Do you provide a warranty?

Yes. We provide a one-year labor warranty on all commercial painting work. Coating manufacturers — Sherwin-Williams, PPG, and Behr — provide their own product warranties (typically 5–15 years depending on the system) that are transferable t/ the building owner. We can provide warranty documentation in a format suitable for your capital improvement files or for submission to your insurance carrier.

Does Southeast work with property management companies and REITs?

Yes, and it's a significant part of our book. We understand the vendor approval process, insurance certificate requirements, and the need for project documentation that satisfies both on-site property managers and asset managers reviewing from corporate. We can work within your existing PO and approval workflow. References from comparable property management accounts are available on request.

What does commercial building painting cost in Atlanta?

Exterior repaint of a commercial building in Atlanta typically runs $1.50–$3.50 per square foot of surface area, depending on substrate condition, coating system, and access method (brush-and-roll vs. spray, ground-level vs. swing-stage). Interior commercial repaints run $0.75–$2.00 per square foot. These are ranges — actual pricing requires a site walk to measure and assess. We provide line-item proposals, not ballpark numbers, so you have a document you can submit for budget approval.
