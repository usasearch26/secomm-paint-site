Common Questions

What Facility Managers Ask Us

Straight answers to the questions we hear most often from property managers and facility directors before they hire us.

How do you handle painting in an occupied building without disrupting our operations?

We start with a pre-job meeting to map out access, occupant flow, noise sensitivity, and any areas that need to stay completely off-limits. From there we sequence the work by zone — one section at a time — and contain dust and overspray before it becomes your problem. If your building needs night or weekend work, that is what we schedule.

Interior Commercial Painting · Office Painting · High-Rise Painting

How do I know your painters are actually experienced commercial crews?

Our painters work commercial and industrial projects exclusively — no residential side work, no day laborers pulled from a hiring lot. Most of our crew members have been with us for years. They know the difference between a tilt-wall warehouse, a CMU block facility, and an occupied office building — and how to approach each one correctly. That experience is not something you can fake on a jobsite.

Contstruction Painting · Warehouse Painting · Line Striping Painting

What's the difference between a professional commercial painter and a general painter?

A general painter knows how to apply paint to a wall. A professional commercial painter knows substrate chemistry, coating system selection, mil thickness requirements, application methods by surface type, OSHA jobsite compliance, and how to work inside an occupied facility without shutting it down. The price difference between the two is real — so is the difference in how long the work lasts and what your building looks like at the end.
